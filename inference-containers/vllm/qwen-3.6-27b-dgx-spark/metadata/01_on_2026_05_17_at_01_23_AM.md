# Analysis:

## Is 2 Worth It vs. 1?

This is the right question. Let's break it down mathematically:

### Speedup Equation

```
Speedup ≈ 1 + (Accepted Tokens / Total Steps)
```

### `num_speculative_tokens: 1`
| Position | Acceptance | Accepted |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | ~80% | **0.80 tokens** |

Overhead: Minimal (single MTP forward pass per step)

### `num_speculative_tokens: 2`
| Position | Acceptance | Accepted |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | ~80% | 0.80 tokens |
| 2 | ~60% | 0.48 tokens *(0.80 × 0.60)* |
| **Total** | | **1.28 tokens** |

Overhead: ~2x MTP forward passes (you run both positions regardless of acceptance)

### Net Comparison

| Config | Accepted/Step | Overhead | Net Benefit |
|--------|---------------|----------|-------------|
| **N=1** | 0.80 tokens | 1 MTP pass | **+0.80** |
| **N=2** | 1.28 tokens | 2 MTP passes | **+0.28 marginal** |

The **marginal gain** of going from N=1 to N=2 is:
- Extra accepted: `1.28 - 0.80 = 0.48 tokens`
- Extra cost: 1 additional MTP pass

**Verdict: The marginal benefit of N=2 over N=1 is small.**

### My Take

**`num_speculative_tokens: 1` is likely the sweet spot** for these MTP heads because:

1. **Position 1 acceptance is high** (~80%) - maximum efficiency
2. **Position 2 drops significantly** (~60%) - diminishing returns
3. **Each additional MTP pass adds fixed overhead** - you pay for it whether accepted or not

### Recommendation

Use **`num_speculative_tokens: 1`** with `qwen3_next_mtp`:

```json
{"method":"qwen3_next_mtp","num_speculative_tokens":1}
```

This gives you the highest acceptance rate with minimal overhead. The theoretical speedup vs. no speculative decoding is ~1.6-1.8x on the generation phase.

**If you want more throughput**, look at other levers:
- Increase `--max-num-seqs` (batching)
- Increase `--max-num-batched-tokens` (prefill throughput)
- Optimize `--gpu-memory-utilization`
