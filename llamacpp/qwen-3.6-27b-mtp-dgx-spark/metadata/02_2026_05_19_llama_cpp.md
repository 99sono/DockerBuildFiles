# analysis 

Interesting results - the aggressive settings actually made things **worse**, not better. Here's the comparison:

## Old vs New Config

| Metric | Old (n=2) | New (n=3, p=0.75) | Change |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| Generation (1st req) | 18.59 tok/s | **16.83 tok/s** | -10% ↓ |
| Generation (2nd req) | 21.83 tok/s | **14.90 tok/s** | -32% ↓ |
| Draft acceptance | 72% → 94% | 64.5% → 53.3% | Degrading faster |

## What went wrong

The lower confidence threshold (0.75) is accepting too many incorrect draft tokens, and with 3-token chains, the verification overhead when a chain fails is significant. The model generates MORE draft tokens (279 vs 68 in the first request), but a higher proportion get rejected, which costs compute.

## Recommendation: Go back to conservative settings

I'd suggest trying:
- `--spec-draft-n-max 2` (back to 2)
- `--spec-draft-p-min 0.85` (higher than the 5090's 0.80, be more selective)

The lesson: on this model with this context size, the MTP heads are already quite accurate (~72-94% acceptance). Being more aggressive doesn't help because it introduces more failed verifications which are expensive. The sweet spot is accepting shorter chains with high confidence rather than longer chains with lower confidence.
